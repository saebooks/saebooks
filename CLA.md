# SAE Books — Contributor Licence Agreement

> *Your books. Your database. Your control.*
>
> This CLA is the agreement every external contributor must sign before
> any pull request can be merged into a SAE Books repository. It is
> modelled on the **Apache Individual Contributor License Agreement
> (ICLA) v2.2** with edits to suit SAE Books' dual-licensing model
> (AGPL-3.0 + commercial). Where this document is silent, the Apache
> ICLA's intent applies.
>
> A separate **Corporate CLA (CCLA)** covers contributions made by
> employees on behalf of an employer. The CCLA is requested when a
> contributor identifies their employer as the rights-holder; until the
> CCLA is signed, that contributor cannot merge.

## How to sign

We use the **CLA Assistant** (`cla-assistant.io`) bot to record CLA
signatures against GitHub accounts. The bot comments on your first
pull request with a link to a one-click signing flow. The signed
agreement is stored against your GitHub identity and covers all
subsequent contributions to any SAE Books repository.

If you contribute outside GitHub (e.g. by emailing a patch), reply to
this CLA's text quoting the line:

> *"I have read and agree to the SAE Books Contributor Licence
> Agreement v1.0."*

include your full legal name, contact email, and (optionally) GitHub
handle, and send to `cla@saebooks.com.au`. We log it manually.

## Corporate CLA (employer rights-holder)

If your employer owns the intellectual property in your contributions
(typical for full-time employees contributing during work hours), an
authorised representative of the employer must sign the **Corporate
CLA** in addition to your individual CLA. The CCLA wording is the same
as this document with the addition of an employer signatory and a
list of designated employee contributors. Email
`cla@saebooks.com.au` to request the CCLA template.
